What is GBV?

Gender-based Violence (GBV) refers to a harmful act inflicted on individuals based on gender, including physical, sexual, psychological, and economic abuse, often rooted in power imbalances and societal norms.

Educational Resources

Explore GBV, its root causes, and prevention strategies.

Effects of GBV

  • Physical and Mental Health Issues – Survivors may suffer injuries, chronic pain, anxiety, depression, PTSD, and even suicide risk.
  • Economic Impact – GBV can lead to loss of income, job instability, and financial dependence due to physical or emotional trauma.
  • Social Consequences – Survivors may face stigma, isolation, or strained relationships with family and friends.
  • Intergenerational Effects – Children exposed to GBV are more likely to experience emotional distress, perform poorly in school, or perpetuate the cycle of violence.

How You Can Help

You can help end GBV by spreading awareness, supporting survivors, and reporting cases of abuse.
